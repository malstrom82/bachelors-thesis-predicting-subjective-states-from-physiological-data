# Physiological Signal Analysis for Driver State Monitoring

## Overview

This repository contains the work conducted as part of a Bachelor’s thesis in Cognitive Science / Artificial Intelligence.

The project investigates how physiological signals can be used to infer driver states in a simulated driving environment. The goal is to explore whether measurable biological signals can provide insight into subjective conditions such as stress, attention, or cognitive load, with potential applications in traffic safety and driver monitoring systems.

---

## Motivation

Driver impairment due to stress, fatigue, or reduced attention is a major factor in traffic accidents. Traditional monitoring approaches rely on behavioral observation or self-reporting, which are limited in real-time applications.

Physiological signals offer a potential alternative by providing continuous, objective measurements of internal states. However, interpreting these signals reliably presents challenges due to noise, variability, and individual differences.

This project explores how such signals can be analyzed and structured to extract meaningful information about driver state.

---

## Objectives

The main objectives of this thesis are:

- To collect and process physiological data in a controlled driving simulation  
- To identify relevant features associated with changes in driver state  
- To explore relationships between physiological signals and subjective or task-related conditions  
- To evaluate the feasibility of using such signals for real-time monitoring  

---

## Methodology

The project combines experimental data collection with signal analysis:

### Data Collection
- Simulated driving environment  
- Controlled experimental conditions  
- Recording of physiological signals (e.g., heart rate, response times, or related measures)  

---

### Signal Processing
- Preprocessing and cleaning of raw signals  
- Extraction of time-domain and/or frequency-domain features  
- Handling of noise and signal variability  

---

### Analysis
- Comparison across experimental conditions  
- Identification of patterns linked to cognitive or emotional states  
- Exploration of correlations between signals and task performance  

---

## Key Insights

The study highlights several important aspects of physiological signal analysis:

- Physiological signals contain meaningful information about internal states, but interpretation is non-trivial  
- Signal variability and noise require careful preprocessing and feature selection  
- Relationships between signals and subjective states are often indirect and context-dependent  
- Reliable inference requires both technical processing and experimental design considerations

## Limitations

- Limited dataset size and experimental scope  
- Simplified simulation environment compared to real-world driving  
- Challenges in generalizing results across individuals  
- No fully automated classification system implemented
